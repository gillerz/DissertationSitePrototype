Keeping Things Up To Date


Keeping your devices, and the apps and software on them is a key part of keeping yourself protected from various threats. Be it system software updates that patch up bugs or security updates that fix vulnerabilities - they are vital to ensure your protection and prolongthe life of your device.

Updating your Operating System

You should rarely ever have to update your operating system, as they are done automatically - you may have probably already gotten a notification before from your device telling you it needs to restart in order to finalise installing the update. However, in the event you do want to doublecheck, here’s how:
Windows: go to settings, scroll down on the sidebar to see “Windows Update” - there you will see if you have the options to either search for updates or install an update you may have missed.
Mac: press the Apple menu icon, then go into System Preferences. From there click on Software Update - there you will have an option that says either “Update Now” or “Upgrade Now”. “Update” installs an update for your current version of MacOS; and “upgrade” installs the newest version of MacOS.

Upgrading to whatever is the current version is always recommended for your own protection.
